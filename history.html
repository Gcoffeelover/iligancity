The History of Iligan City

Iligan City, often referred to as the "City of Majestic Waterfalls," has a rich cultural and historical heritage that dates back centuries.





The Early Beginnings


Before the Spanish colonization, Iligan was home to various indigenous groups, including the Higaunon, Maranao, and other Lumad communities. The area was known for its rich natural resources, particularly the rivers and waterfalls that played a vital role in the lives of the early inhabitants.


The Spanish Era


During the Spanish period, the region came under the influence of Spanish missionaries, particularly in the establishment of the town of Iligan. The city's history was marked by its role as a strategic settlement for the Spanish forces in the northern Mindanao region.

American and Japanese Occupation

In the early 20th century, during the American colonial period, Iligan underwent modernization, with the establishment of infrastructure such as roads, schools, and government offices. The Japanese occupation during World War II left significant scars on the city, with many structures being damaged or destroyed.


Post-War Development

After the war, Iligan began to rebuild and prosper. The establishment of industries such as the National Power Corporation (NPC), hydroelectric plants, and agricultural developments further boosted the city's economy. Iligan transformed into a key city in northern Mindanao, known for its picturesque landscapes and bustling urban growth.


Modern Iligan

Today, Iligan City is a bustling urban center that retains its historical charm. It is a city that values its cultural heritage, visible in its preserved landmarks, festivals, and the hospitality of its people. The city's natural beauty, highlighted by its waterfalls and springs, continues to be a major tourist attraction. Iligan remains a melting pot of diverse cultures and traditions, contributing to its unique identity.
