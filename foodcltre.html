Food and Culture in Iligan City

Cheding's Peanut

Cheding's Peanut is a famous Iligan delicacy known for its crunchy, sweet, and savory flavor. It is made from roasted peanuts coated with caramelized sugar and a hint of salt, making it a perfect snack for locals and visitors alike. It's a must-try treat when visiting Iligan City!

Suman sa Lihiya

Suman sa Lihiya is a traditional Filipino delicacy made from glutinous rice and cooked with lye water, wrapped in banana leaves. It's often served with sugar or coconut milk, and is a popular snack or dessert in Iligan City.

La Paz Batchoy

La Paz Batchoy is a savory noodle soup that originated from Iloilo, but has become a beloved dish in Iligan City. It features a flavorful broth with pork, beef, and a variety of garnishes such as chicharrón, garlic, and green onions.

Cultural Events in Iligan City

Iligan City Diyandi Fiesta

The Iligan City Fiesta is an annual cultural and religious celebration that showcases the city’s rich traditions and heritage. The event is highlighted by a grand parade, street dancing, and local exhibitions of arts, crafts, and culinary delights.

Pasundayag Festival

The Pasundayag Festival is a vibrant cultural festival celebrating Iligan's history, indigenous communities, and creative arts. It’s a time for the locals to showcase their traditional dances, music, and colorful costumes.

Ma. Cristina Anyo Festival

The Ma. Cristina Anyo Festival celebrates the vibrant culture and heritage of Iligan's Ma. Cristina Falls and the surrounding area. This festival features cultural performances, environmental awareness programs, and sports competitions.
